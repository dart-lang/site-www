Google Summer of Code (GSoC) is a global program focused on bringing student developers into open source software development. Students are sponsored by Google to work with a mentoring open source organization on a 10-week programming project during the summer. Over the past 16 years, more than 16,000 students have participated in Google Summer of Code.

Weâ€™re excited to announce that Dart will be a mentoring organization in [Google Summer of Code 2021](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/). This will be the second time Dart participates in Google Summer of Code. In 2020, the Dart organization mentored [5 successful students](https://medium.com/dartlang/google-summer-of-code-2020-results-a38cd072c9fe).

## Are you interested?

Get started now by reading [the list of project ideas](https://github.com/dart-lang/sdk/wiki/Dart-GSoC-2021-Project-Ideas) to find a match for your skills and interests. Although formal applications [open on March 29th](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/how-it-works/), you can discuss and adapt project ideas with prospective mentors today.
> The Dart team expects to have enough mentors to accept only a handful of applications, so also check out the [other mentoring organizations](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/organizations/).

If you have questions specific to Dart and GSoC, ask them on [our dedicated mailing list](https://groups.google.com/forum/#!forum/dart-gsoc).

For more information about Google Summer of Code, watch the video below. Or read the[ student guide for Google Summer of Code](https://google.github.io/gsocguides/student/).
